## Overview

The Nanoparticle Metrology Group develops and applies advanced measurement methods for the characterization of nanoparticles in complex biological and environmental matrices. Our work bridges fundamental nanoscience with practical applications in diagnostics, nanomedicine, and environmental monitoring.

## Research Topics

### Nanoparticle Size and Concentration Measurements
We develop and validate methods for accurate measurement of nanoparticle size distributions and absolute concentrations, using techniques such as Nanoparticle Tracking Analysis (NTA), Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS), and Single Particle Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry (spICP-MS).

### Extracellular Vesicle Characterization
Extracellular vesicles (EVs), including exosomes and microvesicles, are nanoscale particles shed by cells that play important roles in cell-to-cell communication and disease. We develop quantitative methods to characterize EVs for biomarker discovery and diagnostics.

### Surface Chemistry and Functionalization
Understanding the surface chemistry of nanoparticles is critical for their application in biosensing and nanomedicine. We investigate how surface coatings and functionalization affect nanoparticle stability, interactions with biological systems, and detection performance.

### Reference Materials and Metrology Standards
We contribute to the development of reference materials and metrological standards for nanoparticle characterization, supporting traceability and comparability of measurements across laboratories.

## Techniques and Equipment

- Nanoparticle Tracking Analysis (NTA)
- Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS)
- Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)
- Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)
- Flow Cytometry
- Single Particle ICP-MS (spICP-MS)
- Fluorescence Correlation Spectroscopy (FCS)
